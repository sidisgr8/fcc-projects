Grace Hopper: The Grandmother of Computing

A Remarkable Life and Legacy

Grace Brewster Murray Hopper was a groundbreaking computer scientist, mathematician, and United States Navy rear admiral who fundamentally transformed the world of computing. Born on December 9, 1906, in New York City, she was one of the first programmers of the Harvard Mark I computer and invented the first compiler for a computer programming language.

Key Achievements

  • Developed the first compiler for a computer programming language
  • Pioneered the concept of machine-independent programming languages
  • Coined the term "debugging" after removing a moth from a computer
  • Advocated for the development of a programming language that could use English-like statements

Notable Quotes

"It's easier to ask forgiveness than it is to get permission."
"The most dangerous phrase in the language is, 'We've always done it this way.'"
